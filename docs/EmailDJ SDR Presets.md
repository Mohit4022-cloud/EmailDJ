# **EmailDJ: The SDR "Remix Studio" Presets**

**Ranked by Outbound Frequency (Highest Volume to Lowest Volume)**

### **1\. The Straight Shooter**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#1 (High Volume Daily Use)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Top 40 / Pop-Rock  
* **The Vibe:** Clear, concise, and purely functional. Used for standard, high-volume persona-based campaigns to state what you do, who you do it for, and ask for interest. No gimmicks.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 60% Formal (Professional, standard B2B tone)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 50% Center (Classic "Tired of X? We give you Y.")  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 40% Short (Respects their inbox, gets straight to the point)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 60% Bold (Direct call-to-action: "Open to a quick chat?")  
* **Why it works:** In a world of overly clever emails, clarity wins. This preset is designed for scale to find out if there is a project currently on their desk that matches your solution.

### **2\. The Headliner**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#2 (High Volume Daily Use)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Stadium Anthem / High Energy  
* **The Vibe:** FOMO-driven and metric-heavy. The "Social Proof" play. Used when targeting a prospect by dropping the names of their direct competitors or industry peers who are using your product to win.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 40% Casual (Approachable but confident)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 90% Outcome-Led (Focus on the massive ROI)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 60% Long (Needs just enough room to tell a mini case-study)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 80% Bold ("We helped \[Competitor\] achieve \[Metric\].")  
* **Why it works:** Buyers are competitive. If a VP hears that a rival just increased revenue by 12% using your tool, they have to take the meeting out of self-preservation.

### **3\. The Giver**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#3 (Medium-High Volume)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Lo-Fi / Chill Beats  
* **The Vibe:** Helpful, zero-pressure, and value-first. The "Deposit before Withdrawal" play. Used when offering a lead magnet, a free custom audit, or a Loom video.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 80% Casual (Friendly, non-threatening)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 60% Problem-Led ("Noticed a tiny gap...")  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 30% Short (Focus entirely on the free asset)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 100% Diplomatic ("Mind if I send it over? No worries if not.")  
* **Why it works:** It dramatically lowers the barrier to entry. It is much harder to say "no" to a free, customized resource that makes them look good at their job than a 30-minute meeting.

### **4\. The Challenger**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#4 (Medium Volume)  
* **EQ Vibe:** High Distortion / Rock  
* **The Vibe:** Disruptive, provocative, and educational. Used when you need to wake a prospect up to a macro-problem they didn't know they had or challenge their status quo.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 60% Casual (Conversational, like a peer)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 100% Problem-Led (Poke the bear, highlight the pain)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 40% Short (Needs a little space to explain the "danger")  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 100% Bold (Challenging their worldview)  
* **Why it works:** It agitates a specific pain point to create urgency. It’s less about being overly polite and more about making the prospect say, "Wait, is my company at risk?"

### **5\. The Industry Insider**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#5 (Medium Volume, Trigger-Based)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Spoken Word / Podcast  
* **The Vibe:** Insightful, highly customized, and heavily researched. Used when leveraging a trigger event (news, earnings call) or deep account research.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 50% Center (Professional, but reads like a knowledgeable peer)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 60% Problem-Led (Focuses on an external industry shift)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 75% Long (Needs breathing room to synthesize the research)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 85% Diplomatic ("Thought this might be relevant...")  
* **Why it works:** Buyers love learning about their own industry. This preset is less about "pitching a product" and more about sharing a well-researched perspective, perfectly utilizing your Deep Research tool.

### **6\. The Icebreaker**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#6 (Medium-Low Volume, Trigger-Based)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Pop / Upbeat & Light  
* **The Vibe:** Warm, highly personalized, and low-pressure. Best used for "warm" outbound (downloaded an ebook, mutual connection) or highly creative pattern-interrupts.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 100% Casual (Friendly, human-to-human)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 70% Outcome-Led (Focuses on exciting possibilities)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 30% Short (Breezy)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 70% Diplomatic (Very low-friction Call to Action)  
* **Why it works:** It strips away the "sales breath." It’s the equivalent of a friendly tap on the shoulder at a networking event rather than a megaphone presentation.

### **7\. The Trusted Advisor**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#7 (Low Volume, Persona-Specific)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Classical / Balanced Theater  
* **The Vibe:** Consultative, thorough, and safe. Used for mid-level managers, directors, or technical buyers who need to understand *how* things work and avoid risk.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 80% Formal (Highly professional)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 50% Center (Balances the pain with the promised land)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 80% Long (Provides context, proof points, and mapped services)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 90% Diplomatic (Soft suggestions, helpful tone)  
* **Why it works:** Managers buy differently than CEOs. They need details to justify taking a meeting to their boss. This preset gives them the meat on the bones without backing them into a corner.

### **8\. The C-Suite Sniper**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#8 (Low Volume, Persona-Specific)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Bass-Heavy & Punchy  
* **The Vibe:** Ruthlessly efficient, respectful of time, and confident. Used for CEOs, CFOs, and Founders who care about business risks and the bottom line, not features.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 70% Formal (Professional but not stiff)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 80% Outcome-Led (Focus on the ROI / strategic win)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 100% Short (Get in, make the point, get out)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 80% Bold (Direct ask, no fluff)  
* **Why it works:** Executives skim emails on their phones between meetings. They need to see the value immediately. This preset strips away pleasantries and gets straight to the bold business case.

### **9\. The Visionary**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#9 (Situational, Strategic)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Orchestral / Cinematic  
* **The Vibe:** Inspiring, big-picture, and narrative-driven. Used mostly by startups selling disruptive tech (like AI) to paint a picture of a changing world.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 50% Center (Eloquent but accessible)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 100% Outcome-Led (Focus on the promised land)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 90% Long (Requires a narrative arc)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 50% Center (Inviting them on a journey)  
* **Why it works:** When selling something entirely new (category creation), you can't sell features; you have to sell a new way of working. This preset gives the AI the room it needs to weave a compelling story.

### **10\. The VIP Pass**

* **Outbound Frequency:** \#10 (Situational, Highly Targeted)  
* **EQ Vibe:** Jazz / Smooth & Coordinated  
* **The Vibe:** Navigational, deferential, and highly contextual. The multi-threading play. Used when referencing a shared investor, board member, or internal referral.  
* **Slider Settings:**  
  * **Formal ↔ Casual:** 80% Formal (Respecting corporate hierarchy)  
  * **Problem-Led ↔ Outcome-Led:** 70% Outcome-Led (Focusing on the broad company goal)  
  * **Short ↔ Long:** 20% Short (Just state the connection and the hand-off)  
  * **Bold ↔ Diplomatic:** 90% Diplomatic ("Your VP asked me to connect...")  
* **Why it works:** It leverages borrowed authority. It requires a specific trigger, but when an SDR has that warm intro, they need a preset that is incredibly smooth and brief so they don't blow the opportunity.
